The Heritage Houses

Four ateliers.
One standard.

We stock only these four houses. Not by limitation — by conviction.

I.

Delvaux

Est. 1829 — Brussels

The world's oldest fine leather goods house. Every Brillant, every Tempête, is assembled by a single artisan over several days. The lock mechanism alone has more moving parts than any competitor.

Known to those who know.
II.

Valextra

Est. 1937 — Milan

Pure geometry. No external pockets. No visible stitching. No logo on the exterior. A Valextra piece is recognisable only to someone who has touched one. That is intentional. That is the point.

Architecture in leather.
III.

Loro Piana

Est. 1924 — Quarona

Holders of the world's finest cashmere and vicuña sourcing rights. A Loro Piana coat has no discernible branding. The price tag — once removed — leaves only the fabric itself. And the fabric is enough.

The fabric is the flex.
IV.

Moynat

Est. 1849 — Paris

Predating Louis Vuitton by four years, Moynat's curved trunks sailed on ocean liners and trains across Empire-era Europe. Revived with utmost discretion, each piece now carries that entire history silently.

History without announcement.

Our Standard

Not a marketplace.
A point of view.

eBay has 1.7 billion listings. Poshmark has millions of unverified sellers. We have a selection. The difference is not scale — it is judgement.

i.

Every piece is examined in person

No platform uploads. No seller descriptions. Our team physically handles each acquisition before it is listed. Hardware tested. Leather assessed. Provenance confirmed.

ii.

We decline more than we accept

Roughly 70% of pieces submitted for consignment are declined. Condition, rarity, and house alignment all matter. We would rather offer nothing than offer something mediocre.

iii.

Stealth, not status

Our buyers are not buying labels. They are buying craft. They are buying the knowledge that separates them — quietly, invisibly, permanently — from those who need to announce it.

iv.

Access, not volume

We operate by enquiry and private list. No checkout button. No cart. Acquisition at PROVENANCE begins with a conversation. We prefer it that way.

How It Works

Buy or Consign

I Request.

Request Access

Submit your enquiry below. We respond to every serious request within one business day. Browsing is free. There is no obligation.

II Select.

Select Your Piece

Browse the current collection. When a piece resonates, enquire. We send full condition notes, additional photographs, and the complete file.

III Acquire.

Secure & Ship

Payment is settled directly — no platform fees deducted. The piece is wrapped, documented, and dispatched with full insurance and tracking.

IV Own.

Arrives Perfectly

Every shipment includes the complete authentication dossier, condition record, and PROVENANCE certificate. The piece is now yours to keep — or to return to us one day.

I Submit.

Submit for Review

Describe your piece and send photographs. We assess acquisition fit, condition, and pricing within 48 hours. No obligation at this stage.

II Send.

Ship to Our Atelier

If accepted, we send a prepaid, fully insured shipping label. The piece travels to us under our insurance for the duration of consignment.

III List.

We Curate & List

Our team photographs, writes, and lists your piece. We set the price together, based on market data and condition assessment. No guessing.

IV Receive.

You Are Paid

When the piece sells, you receive 75% of the final sale price — transferred within 3 business days of the buyer confirming receipt.
