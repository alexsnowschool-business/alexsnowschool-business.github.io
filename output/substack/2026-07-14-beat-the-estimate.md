# Sotheby's sellers cash in on a 140-percent surge

*This week's remarkably consistent overperformance suggests collectors are bidding aggressively across centuries and mediums alike.*

---

This week's overperformers clustered almost entirely at Sotheby's, with six works clearing their low estimates by 137 to 141 percent—a narrow, suspicious band that suggests less a market signal than a venue effect. The estimates themselves ranged from $2,000 to $15,000, spanning Old Masters, twentieth-century abstraction, and decorative arts, indicating no single category or artist tier drove the surge. When the estimate—the specialist's calibrated prediction of where the market will bid—gets beaten this consistently and this uniformly, the gap warrants scrutiny. Such clustering can reflect fresh buyer competition, weak initial estimates, or seasonal lot selection rather than broad market appetite. The concentration at a single house also limits what we can infer about dealer and collector behavior more widely. The data here describes an outlier week, not a trend.
