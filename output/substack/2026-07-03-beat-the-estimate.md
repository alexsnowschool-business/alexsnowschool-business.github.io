# Romantic landscapes soar past estimates at Sotheby's

*Buyers show renewed appetite for 19th-century European figurative and landscape painting, driving consistent outperformance across the category.*

---

This week's five overperformers share an unusual concentration: all cleared the block at Sotheby's, all landed in the $20K–$241K range, and all crossed the low estimate by 141 to 179 percent. The clustering itself is the signal. When a single house produces this depth of outperformance across unrelated artists and periods—a Welsh landscape painter, a Victorian illustrator, a Danish symbolist, Russian nineteenth-century names—it suggests either a particularly well-calibrated estimate round or a room that moved decisively in one direction. The estimate, remember, represents the specialist's thesis about where the market has settled; a gap this wide indicates material disagreement between the estimate and the bidders who showed up. Whether that disagreement reflects newfound appetite for these specific artists, a broader shift in pre-sale pricing, or simply a strong sale day remains the open question. The data here documents the divergence; context will determine its meaning.
