# Sotheby's delivers a masterclass in conservative estimates

*When pre-sale valuations cluster this tightly, collectors aren't just bidding—they're voting with conviction.*

---

This week's overperformers reveal a concentrated signal: all five works crossed the block at Sotheby's, and all cleared their low estimates by precisely 36 to 38 percent. The clustering matters less for its uniformity than for what it suggests about estimate-setting. The low estimate functions as a specialist's thesis about where bidders will engage; when five separate lots—spanning from natural history illustration to royal portraiture, from $6,000 to $150,000—each exceed that threshold by similar margins, it indicates either systematic underestimation by the house or a market moment where collectors are bidding more aggressively than the pre-sale read anticipated. The gap between estimate and hammer is the market's correction to the specialist's forecast. Understanding whether this week's pattern reflects miscalibration or genuine momentum shift requires looking beyond the numbers themselves to the quality of competition in the room.
