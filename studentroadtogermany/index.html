Safety & Well-being လုံခြုံရေးနှင့် ကျန်းမာရေး

Germany consistently ranks among the world's 10 safest countries, with universal healthcare and strong rule of law. Your child will also be supported by a growing Myanmar student community in every major German city.

II

Financial Transparency ငွေကြေးပွင့်လင်းမြင်သာမှု

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Most German universities require a minimum GPA of 3.0/4.0 or equivalent. However, requirements vary by university and program. We evaluate your specific profile and find universities that match your academic background during the free consultation.

A Sperrkonto is a blocked bank account required by the German embassy/consulate to prove you have sufficient funds (€11,208/year as of 2024) to support yourself during your studies. Popular providers include Fintiba and Expatrio. Our blocked account package guides you through the entire setup process.

The typical timeline from starting your application to arriving in Germany is 6–12 months. University applications usually have deadlines 6 months in advance. Visa processing takes 6–12 weeks after submission. We help you plan and track all deadlines to stay on schedule.

Not necessarily. There are over 1,500 English-taught Master's programs in Germany. For German-taught programs, you'll need at least B2 level (TestDaF or DSH exam). We help you identify programs that match your current language level and advise on language courses if needed.

Most public German universities have no tuition fees for international students. You only pay a semester contribution of approximately €150–€350 per semester, which usually includes a public transport pass. Private universities do charge tuition. We always prioritise public universities to minimise your costs.

Visa rejection is rare with proper preparation. If a rejection occurs due to document issues on our end, our Full Service Package includes a full refund guarantee. We also support the re-application or appeal process at no additional charge.

Germany is one of the safest countries in the world for international students. German cities have low crime rates, excellent public transport, and well-resourced student services. Your child will also have access to free healthcare under student health insurance. We connect every student with the local Myanmar student association upon arrival so they are never isolated.

A realistic first-year budget includes: blocked account deposit (€11,208), student health insurance (approx. €120/month), accommodation (€400–600/month), and daily living (€300–400/month). Tuition at public universities is free. We prepare a personalised cost plan for every family during the free consultation so you can plan ahead with full clarity.
