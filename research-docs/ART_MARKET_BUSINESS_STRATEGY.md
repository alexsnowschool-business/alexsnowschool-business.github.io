## II. Brand Identity

### Name
**The Hammer Price**

The hammer price is the final price confirmed at the moment the auctioneer's gavel strikes — the most important number in the art market. The name is self-explanatory to anyone who encounters it, signals domain expertise immediately, and holds up at every tier of the business from TikTok content to a $2,000/month advisory retainer.

### Handle
`@thehammerprice` — claim on TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube before posting anything.

### Positioning
The data-backed, accessible voice in the art auction market. Not a gallery. Not a consultant. Not an academic journal. A creator who shows their working in public and builds a track record over time.

### Tone
- **TikTok:** Fast, surprising, slightly provocative — "The auction house doesn't want you to know this"
- **Instagram:** Considered, aesthetic, data-informed — more Economist than BuzzFeed
- **Newsletter:** Clear, direct, one strong insight per issue
- **Advisory:** Confident, precise, no jargon

## III. Visual Identity

### Logo Concept

Two components: a standalone **icon** (for profile pictures and small formats) and a full **wordmark** (for newsletter headers, documents, and any future site).

**Icon — minimal gavel strike mark**

Not a realistic gavel (reads as law firm). A single clean geometric downstroke: a short thick horizontal bar capping a vertical stem. Abstract enough to feel modern, specific enough to read as auction. Two shapes. No gradients. Works at 110×110px cropped to a circle.


**Wordmark — "THE HAMMER PRICE" in two weights**

- "THE" — small caps, light weight, tracked out
- "HAMMER PRICE" — larger, medium or bold weight, caps
- Stacked or inline depending on format

**Recommended fonts:**
- Cormorant Garamond (already in the project design system) — editorial, elegant
- DM Serif Display — slightly more modern
- Instrument Serif — clean and contemporary

### Colour Palette

| Role | Colour | Hex |
|---|---|---|
| Primary | Near-black | `#1A1A1A` |
| Accent | Warm gold | `#C9A84C` |
| Background | Ivory | `#F5F0E8` |
| Secondary background | White | `#FFFFFF` |

The gold is intentional — Christie's and Sotheby's both lean into gold as a signal of the auction world. Owning that colour in the social space is a positioning move. The ivory softens the palette and keeps it warm rather than corporate.

### What to Avoid
- Realistic gavel illustrations — reads legal, not art
- Blue or green — too generic fintech
- Thin hairline fonts — illegible at small sizes on social
- Busy layouts — destroyed by circle crop at profile picture size

### Interim Profile Picture — Lichtenstein: Masterpiece (1962)

Until the logo is ready, use **Roy Lichtenstein's *Masterpiece* (1962)** as the profile picture on both TikTok and Instagram.

**Why this painting specifically:**
- It is literally about the art market — a collector telling an artist "this painting is a MASTERPIECE! My, soon you'll have all of New York clamoring for your work!" The subject matter does double duty as brand commentary
- Close two-figure composition survives the 110×110px circle crop
- Bold outlines and Ben-Day dots remain legible at thumbnail size
- Yellow/black/white palette — high contrast, no ambiguity at small size
- Recognisable as Lichtenstein without being Whaam! or Drowning Girl (both overused)

**Considered and rejected:**
- *Paris Street; Rainy Day* (Caillebotte, 1877) — beautiful but fails at small size (complex wide composition becomes grey blur in a circle), and signals Impressionism rather than the Post-War Contemporary market the data covers. Better used as a newsletter header or banner image where the full composition can breathe
- *Whaam!* — landscape format, terrible circle crop
- *Drowning Girl* — wrong emotional signal for a market intelligence brand

**Copyright note:** Lichtenstein died in 1997 — his work is under copyright until 2067. Using a reproduction as a social media profile picture on a personal account is not something that is pursued legally in practice, but it is not public domain. Swap for the actual logo once it is ready.

**Backup options if Masterpiece doesn't feel right:**
- *Brushstroke* (1965) — single bold yellow stroke, extremely graphic, works at any size
- *I Can See the Whole Room* (1961) — circular peephole composition, naturally suits the circle crop, underused

## IV. Business Concept

**Creator-led art market intelligence service.**

Build a public audience using free content on TikTok and Instagram, grounded in real transaction data. Monetise through a tiered model: newsletter → advisory → retainer.

The data is the differentiator. Most art market commentary is either pure aesthetics (no numbers) or academic reports priced out of reach ($5,000+/year for ArtTactic, Artprice). There is no credible, data-backed, accessible voice in between. The Hammer Price fills that gap.

## VI. Target Audiences

### Tier 1 — Aspiring Collectors (TikTok primary)
- Age 22–35
- Income $80k–$200k
- Curious about art as an alternative asset, watches FinTok
- Has not bought at auction yet but aspires to
- Pain point: the art market feels opaque and inaccessible
- What they want: to feel informed, to spot an opportunity before it's obvious

### Tier 2 — Active Collectors (Instagram primary)
- Age 28–45
- Income $200k+, investable assets $500k+
- Already bought art at gallery level, curious about auction
- Pain point: doesn't know when to buy, when to sell, or which house to use
- What they want: a trusted data source they can act on

### Tier 3 — Adjacent Professionals (newsletter + advisory)
- Interior designers buying on behalf of clients
- Wealth managers whose clients ask about art
- Small galleries tracking the secondary market for artists they represent
- Pain point: no affordable access to market data
- What they want: a credible reference they can show clients

## VII. TikTok Strategy

**Tone:** Fast, surprising, slightly provocative.

**Posting cadence:** 3–4x per week.

### Content Pillars

**1. The Shock Number**
Lead with the outperformance. "This painting had a $35,000 estimate. It sold for $441,000. Here's why no one saw it coming."
> Data hook: above-estimate lots from `art.db`. The Robert Longo +1,160% is the first video.

**2. Pre-Sale Pick**
"This lot comes up at Christie's next week. The estimate is $60,000. Here's why I think the market disagrees."
> Data hook: Artprice futures data for upcoming lots. Follow up with the result. Build a public track record.

**3. The Pattern Reveal**
"51% of lots at Christie's last year sold above estimate. Here's what that actually means for buyers."
> Data hook: `sale_performance` field in `art.db`. Explainable in 60 seconds.

**4. The Cooling Artist**
"Brice Marden sold at 57% below the low estimate. Here's what's happening."
> Data hook: below-estimate lots. Creates contrast with the outperformer content.

**5. The Explainer**
"What does 'estimate' even mean at auction?" — educational content that builds credibility without requiring a specific data hook.

### Growth Tactics
- Stitch / duet other art content creators
- Comment first on Christie's and Sotheby's official posts when a sale goes live
- Post result follow-ups ("I called this last week — here's what happened") to build track record

## VIII. Instagram Strategy

**Tone:** Considered, aesthetic, data-informed.

**Posting cadence:** 4–5x per week across feed + Stories.

### Content Pillars

**1. Data Carousel**
Clean slides. "5 artists who consistently beat their estimates — and what they have in common." Each slide = one artist + stat + one-line insight.
> High save rate. Saves signal to the algorithm.

**2. The Pre-Auction Reel**
Short, direct. "This comes up at Sotheby's Thursday. Estimate: $50,000. My read: underpriced. Here's why."
> Builds anticipation, drives followers to check back for the result.

**3. The Result Post**
Single image or short Reel after a notable sale. "Called it." or "Got this one wrong — here's why."
> Honesty about misses builds more trust than always being right.

**4. Stories: Live Auction Commentary**
During a Christie's or Sotheby's sale, post real-time takes on notable lots.
> High engagement, builds habit of checking Stories during sales.

**5. The Artist Spotlight**
Visual post on one artist — price history, what sold, what's coming up.
> Repurposable across newsletter and advisory materials.

### Growth Tactics
- Tag Christie's, Sotheby's, Artprice on relevant posts
- Use niche hashtags: `#artmarket` `#artcollecting` `#artinvestment` `#auctionresults`
- Collaborate with interior designers and art advisors who have existing audiences
- DM-to-consultation pipeline: engaged followers in the $100k–$500k bracket are warm leads

## IX. Content Engine (How the Data Powers the Calendar)

```
Monday    → Artprice futures: flag upcoming lots this week
Wednesday → Data insight from art.db (pattern, artist, price bracket)
Friday    → Pre-sale pick for the coming week
Saturday  → Result follow-up (if a sale ran this week)
```

The database is a content calendar. Every time the scrapers run, there is new material. The Artprice futures scraper adds pre-sale angles; the Christie's/Sotheby's scrapers add post-sale results.

**The loop that builds the brand:**

```
Upcoming lot → public prediction → sale result → track record
```

After 3 months of this publicly, the advisory product sells itself.
